Tools

FreeCAD was used for computer-aided design (CAD) and finite element method (FEM). The materials and tools required are listed separately for each section.

Steel frame

The steel frame forms the structural backbone of the smart flowerpot, providing support for all attached components. Steel was selected due to its lower cost, higher strength, and greater durability compared to wood. However, a drawback of this material is that it is more challenging to process.

The entire steel structure is made of hollow square profiles measuring 30 × 30 × 3 mm. The material used is S235JRH structural steel. A total of 29 meters of this material is required to manufacture the smart flowerpot structure.

Tools used in the manufacture of the smart fowerpot structure are listed in the Manufacturing section.

CAD

Steel frame assembly is divided into three subassemblies. Each subassembly is composed of several profiles with varying lengths and cut angles. The bill of materials is listed in the Manufacturing section. For the purposes of FEM analysis, the profiles are capped on both ends with thin sheet metal plates.

Base

Base forms the foundation for the entire smart flowerpot and holds the bottom in place. That is why it contains additional reinforcements. It is important that the base is manufactured with the highest possible precision and is properly treated against weather conditions.

Beams

Beams serve to support the roof and secure the walls of the smart flowerpot. Their hollow interior also functions as a container for growing vegetables. Since the longest profiles are used in this part of the structure, the greatest deviations and twisting can be expected, so grinding and bending may be necessary during assembly.

Roof

Steel roof structure is intended for mounting gutters, polycarbonate roofing, and other components of the smart flowerpot. As the roof structure is not subjected to permanent loads, it does not need to be the strongest part of the assembly. However, manufacturing may be more demanding due to the need to cut the steel profiles for the trusses at more complex angles.

Top connecting profiles are positioned below the highest point of the roof truss to ensure alignment between the roof trusses and the top connecting profiles. For further details, see the Manufacturing section.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing section describes the fabrication process of the steel structure of the smart flowerpot. This section is structured into chronological subsections. The required material was purchased as eleven 3-meter-long hollow square profiles with dimensions of 30 × 30 × 3 mm, made of S235JRH structural steel.

The entire production process was carried out under non-ideal conditions. Nevertheless, considering my limited experience with metalworking and the available space and climate conditions, the achieved result can be regarded as satisfactory.

Cutting

Material was first cut to the required lengths, followed by cutting the necessary angles, and subsequently sanded and prepared for welding. The recommended method is to use a band saw with final sanding performed using an angle grinder. However, only an angle grinder was available, so all operations were performed using this tool.

Welding

Entire steel structure of the smart flowerpot was welded. The MMA welding method was selected due to its accessibility. All welds were performed using E6013 rutile electrodes, which are suitable for amateur welding applications. The welds were further ground down using an angle grinder.

During welding, the basic components (base legs, trusses, frames, etc.) were fabricated first. These components were then welded into individual subassemblies - base, beams, and roof. The final welding of all subassemblies resulted in the complete smart flowerpot structure.

Since I am just starting out with welding, I expected the welds to be of poorer quality. That is because I designed the smart flower pot so that no unnecessary stress would be placed on the welds. However, during the production of the smart flower pot, my welding skills improved slightly, and the last welds look quite decent. However, I am not a professional, so I am unable to assess the quality of the welds.

Since it was not possible to place the connecting profiles of the trusses in the highest position of the roof, it was necessary to design them so that the connecting profiles and trusses lay in a common plane. The connecting profiles are also arranged in a zig-zag pattern. The entire solution is designed to ensure the strongest and most reliable fastening of the polycarbonate roofing.
